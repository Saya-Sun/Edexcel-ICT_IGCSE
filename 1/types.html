Types of Digital Devices

Digital devices process data using binary (0 and 1). They range from powerful computers to small embedded systems used in everyday appliances.

1. Personal Computers

Desktop Computers
  • Large and not portable
  • Require external devices
  • Easy to upgrade
  • High performance
Laptops
  • Portable with battery
  • Built-in screen and keyboard
  • Lower performance than desktops
Single-Board Computers
  • Small and affordable
  • Used in education and robotics

2. Mobile Devices

SIM Card: Identifies a user on a mobile network.

Smartphones
  • Apps, internet, touchscreen
  • Shorter battery life
Tablets
  • Larger screens
  • Wi-Fi / SIM support

3. Cameras & Camcorders

  • Lens: Controls light entering the camera
  • Processor: Improves image quality
  • Sensor: Determines resolution

4. Home Entertainment Systems

  • TV resolution: HD, 4K
  • Sound systems improve audio quality
  • Games consoles for gaming
  • Media players stream content

5. Navigation Aids

  • Uses GPS satellites
  • No internet needed for location
  • Internet needed for live updates

6. Smart Devices

  • Control home systems
  • Connected via the internet (IoT)

Exam Tips

  • Always define + explain
  • Use advantages & disadvantages
  • Include keywords like resolution, processor
  • Compare devices clearly
