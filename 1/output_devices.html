🖥️ OUTPUT DEVICES

IGCSE ICT - Complete Detailed Notes for Exam Success

Definition

Output devices are hardware components that present the results of computer processing to the user or to the outside world.

Forms of Output:
  • Electronic display
  • Printed text
  • Video
  • Audio
  • Tactile (physical output)

1. MONITOR

Monitors display output from a computer on a screen.

Features:

  • Screen Size: Measured diagonally in inches
  • Resolution: Measured in pixels (higher = clearer image)
  • Energy Efficiency: Can enter standby mode
  • Touchscreen: Allows both input and output
  • Refresh Rate: Higher rate = smoother visuals

Types:

  • LCD / LED
  • OLED (better quality, expensive)

2. PRINTER

Printers produce hard copy output on paper.

Types of Printers:

a) Dot Matrix Printer
  • Impact printer (hits ribbon)
  • Noisy
  • Can print multiple copies using carbon paper
b) Inkjet Printer
  • Sprays tiny droplets of ink
  • Uses CMYK colors (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Black)
  • High-quality color printing
  • Ink is expensive
c) Laser Printer
  • Uses laser and toner
  • Fast and high-quality
  • Best for large volume printing

Comparison Table:

Feature Inkjet Laser Dot Matrix
Speed Slow Fast Medium
Quality High (color) Very high Low
Noise Quiet Quiet Noisy

3. 3D PRINTER

Creates physical objects from digital designs by adding layers of material.

How it Works:

  • Builds objects layer by layer
  • Uses heated materials (plastic, resin, metal)

Uses:

  • Medical (prosthetics)
  • Engineering parts
  • Car prototypes
  • Food (chocolate, pasta)

Note: Very slow but highly accurate.

4. PLOTTER

Used to produce large, high-quality drawings.

How it Works:

  • Moves a pen across paper
  • Draws precise lines

Used By:

  • Architects
  • Engineers
  • Designers
  • Cartographers

5. DATA PROJECTOR

Projects images onto large screens or walls.

Features:

  • Brightness: Measured in lumens
  • Resolution: Determines image clarity
  • Zoom: Optical (better) and digital
  • Portability: Some are lightweight

Uses:

  • Classrooms
  • Business meetings
  • Home entertainment

6. SPEAKERS

Output sound from a computer.

Types:

  • Stereo Speakers: Left and right channels
  • Surround Sound: Multiple speakers
  • Simple Speakers: Low-quality alerts

Extra Info:

  • Amplifier controls sound levels
  • Used in entertainment systems

7. CONTROL DEVICES (ACTUATORS)

Devices that convert computer signals into physical actions.

Examples:

  • Valves
  • Pistons
  • Motors
  • Heaters
  • Coolers

Uses:

  • Robotics
  • Industrial systems
  • Smart homes
  • Medical technology
⭐ Exam Tip: Output is not only visual or audio — it can be physical action.

🔥 EXAM SUMMARY

  • Output devices present processed data
  • Soft Copy: Monitor, Speakers
  • Hard Copy: Printers
  • Inkjet vs Laser comparison is very important
  • Actuators produce physical output
