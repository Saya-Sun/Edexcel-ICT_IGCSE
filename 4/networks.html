🌐 Networks & Communication

🔗 Device-to-Device Communication
Devices connect directly to each other using wired or wireless methods.
  • No central network required
  • Can be wired (USB) or wireless (Bluetooth)
  • Used for direct data transfer
📊 Table 4.1: Device-to-Device Communication
Method Type Example Use
USB cable Wired Transfer files between computer and phone
Bluetooth Wireless Connect headphones or transfer files
Wi-Fi Direct Wireless Send files between devices without router
NFC Wireless Contactless payment or quick pairing
👉 Device-to-device = direct connection (no central network needed)
📡 Network Communication
A network is created when two or more computers are connected.
  • Allows sharing of data and resources
  • Enables communication between devices
🏫 LAN (Local Area Network)
  • Small geographical area
  • Examples: homes, schools, offices
  • High speed and reliable
🌍 WAN (Wide Area Network)
  • Covers large geographical areas
  • Connects multiple LANs
  • Often uses telecom companies
  • Slower than LAN
👉 The Internet is the largest WAN
📱 PAN (Personal Area Network)
  • Devices connected around one user
  • Examples: phone, smartwatch, laptop
  • Can be wired or wireless
WPAN = Wireless Personal Area Network
📶 Tethering
Sharing a mobile device’s internet connection with other devices.
  • Uses smartphone/tablet as host
  • Can be wired or wireless
  • Other devices share mobile data
👉 Some providers charge extra for tethering
