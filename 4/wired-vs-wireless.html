🔌 Wired & Wireless Communication

🔌 Wired Communication
  • Devices use cables to communicate
  • Many types: USB, Ethernet
  • Often faster and more stable
📊 Table 4.3: Wired Connections
Type Use
USB Connect peripherals, transfer data
Ethernet Connect devices to networks
🔗 USB
  • Common connection type
  • Used for many devices
  • New versions → faster speeds
🌐 Ethernet
  • Used for wired networks
  • Up to 100m cable length
  • Speed improves with new versions
📡 Wireless Communication
  • No cables required
  • Many different technologies
👉 Wi-Fi ≠ Internet (Wi-Fi is just network connection)
📶 1️⃣ Wi-Fi
  • Connects devices to networks
  • Uses IEEE 802.11 standards
  • New versions → faster + longer range
🔵 2️⃣ Bluetooth
  • Short range
  • Lower data transfer than Wi-Fi
  • Requires pairing
  • Used for headphones, keyboards
📊 Table 4.4: Wi-Fi vs Bluetooth
Wi-Fi Bluetooth
Range Long Short
Speed High Low
Use Internet access Devices/peripherals
📱 3️⃣ 3G & 4G
  • Mobile broadband
  • Used when Wi-Fi not available
  • 4G faster than 3G
📡 4️⃣ Infra-red (IR)
  • Short range
  • Needs line of sight
  • Used in remote controls
💳 5️⃣ NFC
  • Very short range
  • Used for payments and travel cards
  • Uses RFID chips
📊 Wired vs Wireless Comparison
Wired Wireless
Cost Cables are cheap for small setups No cables needed, but may require access points
Safety Risk of tripping over cables No physical risk (some concerns about radiation)
Speed Faster than wireless Slower than wired
Stability Less affected by interference Affected by interference and obstacles
Portability Not portable (limited by cables)
May need boosters over long distances 		Portable within signal range
Mess Can look untidy with many cables Tidy (no cables)
Security More secure Less secure (signals can be intercepted)
Maintenance Cables/ports may wear over time Minimal maintenance
🧠 Quick Summary
  • Wired: fast, stable, secure
  • Wireless: flexible, convenient, portable
⚠️ Health Considerations
The World Health Organization (WHO) states that current evidence suggests wireless signals do not cause health issues.
  • Long-term effects are still being researched
  • Children may be more vulnerable
  • Effects may take years to appear
